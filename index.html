IT HELP DESK

The 12-week IT Help Desk program prepares you for high demand careers in IT. You will have the opportunity to learn the latest technologies in a structured, hands-on learning environment giving you the knowledge and skills to prepare for a career in IT.

The courses are led by experienced faculty, and the labs and lectures are based on real world scenarios. In addition to teaching deep technical skills, FVI will provide job placement assistance to help you in your efforts to get a real career started.

The course doesn't have an official start date yet but it will start on July 2016. The campus will be located in overtown. Spaces are filling up fast, secure your spot now!

The course schedule is Monday through Friday from 9am to 1pm. This course is sponsored by CareerSource South Florida and the Miami Community Redevelopment Agency as part of the TechHire Miami initiative.

How it Works

Once you express interest through this website and give us your contact info, you will join the pool of applicants. The Miami Dade Community Redevelopment agency will then select the most qualified candidates to attend this career training. This program is designed for young high school graduates eager to get their career started.

Learn Hot Technologies

We are all about career training for high end careers. We believe education is an investment and we structure a curriculum to give you great value. Our program is focused on skills which can get you a job now: A+, Network+, and Windows Server.

Career Placement Assistance

The Community Redevelopment Agency will collaborate with Florida Vocational Institute's department of career services to help you find a job upon successful completion of the program.

Skills Covered

Equipped with this training, you will be qualified for high-demand entry level jobs in technology which provide a solid first step towards career advancement. The program is split into four 3-week units in which you will learn the following skills:

  • Learn About Computer Hardware

    Understand how computers work and how to recognize their internal components: network cards, processor, motherboard, etc.

  • Introduction to Computer Networking

    Learn the basics of how the internet work and how computers communicate with one another all over the world.

  • Network+

    Learn about information security and prepare for the network+ exam.

  • Windows Server

    Learn the powerful, industry-standard tools that IT professionals use in order to administer big corporate computer systems.
